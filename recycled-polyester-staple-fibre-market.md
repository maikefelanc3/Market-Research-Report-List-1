<p><p>The recycled polyester staple fiber market is divided into two key types: solid fiber and hollow fiber. Solid fiber is characterized by its dense structure, offering durability and strength, making it ideal for a variety of applications, including textiles and upholstery. In contrast, hollow fiber contains air pockets, resulting in lightweight and insulating properties, which enhance thermal retention in garments and bedding. Both types contribute to sustainable practices by utilizing recycled materials, supporting eco-friendly product development and reducing environmental impact.</p></p>
