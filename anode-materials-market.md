<p><p>The anode materials market primarily consists of three types: artificial graphite, natural graphite, and silicon-based anodes. Artificial graphite, synthesized through high-temperature processes, offers excellent electrochemical performance and thermal stability. Natural graphite, derived from mining, is cost-effective and widely used, though it may have variable quality. Silicon-based anodes promise higher energy density and capacity due to silicon's superior lithium-ion storage, but face challenges like expansion during cycling. Each type serves specific applications in batteries, particularly for electric vehicles and energy storage systems.</p></p>
