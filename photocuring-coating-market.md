<p><p>The photocuring coating market includes two primary types: water-based and solvent-based paints. Water-based paints utilize water as a primary solvent, offering low volatile organic compound (VOC) emissions and environmental benefits. They cure rapidly when exposed to light, providing efficient and durable finishes. In contrast, solvent-based paints rely on organic solvents, delivering strong adhesion and robustness, often used in demanding applications. Both types leverage photocuring technology to enhance performance, expedite drying times, and improve surface quality in various industrial sectors.</p></p>
