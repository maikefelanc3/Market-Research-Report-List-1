<p><p>The single-use laparoscopic retriever market is divided into two main types: detachable and non-detachable. Detachable retrievers are designed to allow users to release the retrieved specimen outside the body, promoting efficient handling and reducing the risk of contamination. In contrast, non-detachable retrievers keep the specimen attached until it is safely removed, minimizing the chance of spillage during extraction. Both types offer distinct advantages in surgical settings, aimed at enhancing patient safety and simplifying procedural workflows.</p></p>
